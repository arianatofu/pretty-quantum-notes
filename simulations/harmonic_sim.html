Harmonic Oscillator Simulation

What is this?

This page shows a Quantum Harmonic Oscillator. Imagine a tiny quantum kitty sitting on a springy pink platform. The kitty can move back and forth, but it prefers certain “energy levels” where it can bounce safely.

What the Graph Shows

  • x-axis: Position of the kitty on the spring (left to right)
  • y-axis: How likely the kitty is to be at each spot
  • Each colored curve = a different energy level (n=0 is lowest energy, n=1 is first excited, etc.)

Key Ideas

  • Higher energy = more wiggles in the curve
  • Nodes (where the curve touches zero) = spots kitty is never found
  • Wavefunction height = probability to find the kitty there
  • The kitty likes to “bounce” in certain positions depending on its energy 💫

Try it!

Hover over the curves in the interactive plot below. Toggle energy levels in the legend to see how the kitty’s bouncing changes.

Summary

This simulation helps you see quantum wavefunctions in action for the harmonic oscillator. Think of it as a kitty on a spring in a pink world, bouncing only where quantum mechanics allows!
