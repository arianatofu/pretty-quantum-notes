Particle in a Box Simulation

What is this?

This page shows a Particle in a Box simulation. Imagine a tiny quantum kitty trapped in a pink box. The kitty can't leave the box, so it has to bounce around inside.

What the Graph Shows

  • x-axis: Where the kitty is inside the box (0 = left, 1 = right)
  • y-axis: How likely the kitty is to be at each spot
  • Each colored curve = a different energy level (n=1 is lowest energy, n=2 is first excited, etc.)

Key Ideas

  • Higher energy = more wiggles in the curve
  • Nodes (where the curve touches zero) = spots kitty is never found
  • Wavefunction height = probability to find the kitty there

Try it!

Hover over the curves in the interactive plot below. Toggle energy levels in the legend to see how the kitty’s bouncing changes.

Summary

This cute simulation helps you see quantum wavefunctions in action. Even though quantum mechanics can feel confusing, think of it as a tiny kitty bouncing in a pink box.
