Mathematical Tools

Complex Numbers

Quantum mechanics relies heavily on complex numbers. A general complex number is written as z = x + iy, where i² = -1. Wavefunctions are typically complex-valued.

Linear Algebra Basics

Vectors, matrices, and operations like addition, multiplication, and transpose are essential. Quantum states are represented as vectors in a Hilbert space.

Inner Products

The inner product between two vectors |ψ⟩ and |φ⟩ is written as ⟨φ|ψ⟩. It gives a measure of “overlap” or probability amplitude.

Operators

Observables in quantum mechanics (like position or momentum) are represented as operators. Operators act on vectors (wavefunctions), e.g. Ô|ψ⟩ = λ|ψ⟩ for eigenvectors.

Eigenvalues and Eigenvectors

Many quantum problems reduce to solving the eigenvalue equation Ô|ψ⟩ = λ|ψ⟩. Eigenvalues correspond to measurable quantities, eigenvectors to the corresponding states.

Dirac Bra–Ket Notation

Dirac notation is a compact way to write quantum states: |ψ⟩ is a ket (column vector), ⟨ψ| is a bra (row vector). Inner products are written as ⟨φ|ψ⟩.

Fourier Transforms

Switching between position and momentum representations often uses Fourier transforms: ψ(p) = (1/√(2πħ)) ∫ ψ(x) e^{-ipx/ħ} dx. This is essential for understanding momentum-space wavefunctions.

Summary

These mathematical tools provide the foundation for the rest of quantum mechanics. Mastering them will make learning topics like the Schrödinger equation, angular momentum, and spin much easier!
