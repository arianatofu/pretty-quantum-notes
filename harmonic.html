Quantum Harmonic Oscillator

Introduction

The quantum harmonic oscillator is a model that describes a particle subject to a restoring force proportional to its displacement:

F = -k x

It is a central model in quantum mechanics because many systems (molecules, vibrations, phonons) can be approximated as harmonic oscillators near equilibrium.

Potential Energy

The potential energy of a quantum harmonic oscillator is:

V(x) = 1/2 m ω² x²

Here, m is the mass, and ω is the angular frequency.

Schrödinger Equation

The time-independent Schrödinger equation for this system is:

- (ħ² / 2m) d²ψ/dx² + 1/2 m ω² x² ψ = E ψ

Solutions

The energy levels are quantized and given by:

E_n = ħ ω (n + 1/2), n = 0,1,2,...

The corresponding wavefunctions involve Hermite polynomials:

ψ_n(x) = (m ω / π ħ)^(1/4) (1/√(2^n n!)) H_n(√(m ω / ħ) x) e^(-m ω x² / 2ħ)

Key Concepts

  • Energy levels are evenly spaced: E_{n+1} - E_n = ħω.
  • The ground state energy is nonzero: E_0 = ½ ħω (zero-point energy).
  • Wavefunctions are either symmetric or antisymmetric depending on n.
  • This model helps explain vibrations in molecules and lattice vibrations in solids.

Summary

The quantum harmonic oscillator is one of the most important exactly solvable models in quantum mechanics. It illustrates quantization, zero-point energy, and wavefunction behavior in a simple and elegant way.
